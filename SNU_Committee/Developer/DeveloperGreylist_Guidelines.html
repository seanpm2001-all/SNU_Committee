The developer greylist

Context

We put companies/organizations on a greylist of developers. For example, Google has been on the Greylist since day 1, so extra work has to be done to ensure what they are committing is completely safe and doesn't invade user privacy.

That being said, datamining, device fingerprinting, backdoors, and other privacy-exploitive features will not be accepted, and the developer will receive a strike. If a developer receives 3 strikes, they will be put on a blacklist, and they will no longer be able to have their commits accepted by SNU, unless their company/organization is able to settle it out. Cash settlements are not allowed as a bribe, even if it is every cent that a company/developer has.

New developers who have no history of privacy violation will be on a whitelist. Their scripts will still be checked the same as the rest, but they won't be on a greylist first thing. Developers can be moved to a greylist without warning, and cannot easily be moved back. There are different shades to the greylist, the darker part of it that you are on, the closer you are to being blacklisted.

Being on the greylist alone isn't a bad thing in some cases. For example, organizations and companies that have more workers are entered into a greylist, so that we can support their volunteers/workers better, that way, 1 volunteer can't ruin it for everyone. Single developers can still be put on the greylist for violations.

Why do I let some organizations have a pass? Google, Microsoft, and other companies have tens of thousands of employees, and not all of them are going to do something malicious on purpose. The committee has to make sure that their commits are safe, if violation continues, I will put them on the blacklist. The Jet Grey list is pretty much the "If you mess up again, it is the last straw".

Companies and organizations that aren't listed

I can't investigate every company and organization, so for those that aren't on the greylist, whitelist, or blacklist already, just assume they are on the greylist.

Privacy

The names of volunteers are private for the lists. Developers who haven't registered their name yet cannot be added to the list for privacy reasons. I can add the name of public companies, but private companies, and developers cannot be added. They will instead get an ID assigned to them.

Names already added are of my friends and family that have contributed to me or SNU in the past technically. Currently, last names are unavailable to prevent identification.

Rule breaking tags

Some tags have been added about what rules certain companies broke. These tags are added to better identify what they could be up to.

Currently, the tags include:

[HISTORY OF PRIVACY VIOLATIONS] - When the company/organization violates user privacy in the past or present.

[HISTORY OF SEVERE PRIVACY VIOLATIONS] - When the company/organization violates user privacy in the past or present in a massive way (mass surveillance and data collection)

[HISTORY OF INEFFICIENT SOFTWARE] - When the company/organization has a history of making bloated software.

[PROPRIETARY SOFTWARE] - When the company/organization has a history of making proprietary software. This alone isn't a major offender, but it can make them untrustworthy, and make privacy violations lesser known.

[UNTRUSTWORTHY] - When the company/organization has a history of lying to their customers, employees, and others. This company is harder to trust, so you can't easily take their word without evidence and research.
